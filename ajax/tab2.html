Ever since a child first sees a car, it is his desire to drive one, to own one. With his little toys, he goes on his knees, pushing the car through rooms, revving his imaginary engine and loving every bit of it.

This love for automobiles is in all of us human beings. In varied proportions without doubt but definitely there.

Every little sound an engine makes, the squeal of burning rubber or the different sounds from an exhaust pipe, understanding these is now what could enthral us, members of teamunwired.

We have held exhibitions on cars ( called Wheels in Tathva, tech fest of NIT Calicut) and hope we got younger minds to keep their passion for cars alive and not just let go into the textbooks alone.

Always looking to get our creations to challenge the cutting edge, books can be found piled up and dog-eared, complex equations broken and our own little theories cropping up as we build remote controlled aircrafts and amphibian transporters.

In IIST's technological fest( Conscientia), we got the second and third prize for the amphibian transporter event(Salilsthalchar) and the robotics competition(Bots up). In NIT Trichy, we ended up first in the “NFS Aqua” remote controlled boat event.

Rocketry was our first foray into the field of science as a hobby. It all began with Newton's third law, something we study in elementary school. Simple and beautiful being the concept, rocketry is what motivated us to actually try something out practically for the first time.

Once in college, it is the aerodynamics and the right propellant volume percentages for a fixed pressure that we brood over. Complex equations and pressure diagrams enter the foray. Patience is tested to the limit as we repeat our runs until optimum ratios are obtained.

In rocketry, we have conducted workshops for NIT students, competed and won the runners-up in bottle rocketry competition (Prakshepan) at IIST trivandrum.

Sharing information is vital in an organization. In ours, every action is the culmination of discussions in which each person puts forward his thought. When this is the order of functioning, it can turn into a very informal process, often without people being aware of what is actually happening.

Paathshaala started with the need for sharing a single video file. The potential of having a website dedicated to the sharing of information among fellow students appealed to us, not just in the altruistic manner but also since it would help learn the ways of the web

Paathshaala a place to learn and share information.It is a content sharing repository inside NIT Calicut. This project won a national database design competition by linux for you magazine. The project is opensourced and the source can be seen at github.
